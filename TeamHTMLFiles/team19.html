Project Description

HemoTrek presents a compact, efficient, and user-friendly blood viscometer designed to diagnose Hyperviscosity Syndrome (HVS) in clinical settings. HVS is a serious medical condition characterized by abnormally viscous blood, which can lead to severe complications such as organ dysfunction and neurological impairments. Early detection and continuous monitoring are crucial for effective treatment; however, current viscometry tools are time-consuming and dependent on centralized laboratory testing, causing delays in patient care. HemoTrek provides clinicians with a point-of-care solution that delivers real-time viscosity measurements to enable timely interventions.

Takeaways

There will be so many unexpected obstacles that you will encounter with any techincal work you do. No matter what, there will always be a solution or alternative approach. Working together as a team is crucial to making progress, but trusting each other's individual work is the key to completing projects on time.

Advice for Future BME Students

Start prototyping early! Having a partial prototype to show by the end of 4A will help convey your vision for the project to the teaching team, allowing you to receive better feedback and clarify expectations. You will also encounter significantly more problems than you expect, so don't rule out any potential solution too early.
